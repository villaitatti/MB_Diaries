We looked at Filippino and Ghirlandaio in the Pitti and Uffizi in the morning. Costa joined in. He seemed dreadfully depressed.

In the afternoon I went down to write quietly with B., as the four children and smoky fires made it quite uninhabitable here. But Loeser came in and had tea and interrupted us.

* Thursday, February 18, 1892, Florence

Fra Bartolommeo, Granacci, Ridolfo Ghirlandaio in the morning, also Raffaelo Botticini.

We lunched with Loeser, who gave us a capital lunch.

Musgrave came in the afternoon and while B. read Herrick,

he dictated to me the first Canti of his translation of Dante, which I did for him on the type-writer.

We looked at photographs, and B. almost decided that the so-called Signorelli fresco in the Sistine Chapel is by Fiorenzo di Lorenzo.

Finished our Correggio and sent it off to Cram.

I wrote more of my story.
