* Friday Nov. 27. ’91. Pension Lejeune. Vienna
We went to the Gallery in the morning and saw the Dürers and the Holbeins first, and then went on to the Italians.
What a curious trait of  intellectual dependence  – or rather intellectual adrift-ness, one keeps coming across in English people! They seem so uneducated, so little alive, so clinging to the one intellectual straw they have got hold of, that when you upset their hobby, or take away their straw, they cry out, as if they are drowning. “O what shall I do? What can I catch hold of?” They often leave Oxford as helpless, intellectually, as children, if they are honest people.
