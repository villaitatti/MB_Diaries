 Monday May 2. ‘92 Hotel Pellegrino. Bologna
Arrived at Florence 10.30. Bank, etc., etc. Said goodbye to Gertrude. It was hard to listen to her vapourings about Julia’s “sympathy with childhood,” etc., etc., when all the women of the Pension had besieged me on the stairs to tell me that Julia was very cruel and kept the children crying all day long!We came on at 2.30 to Bologna, with some nice Americans, and had a little look around the splendid town.
*  Tuesday May 3. ’92. Bologna.
Churches in the morning and the Gallery in the afternoon. Then a little rest (for I was dead tired) and another stroll - Saw Palazzo Bevilacqua.Bernhard is reading  Michelet’s  History of Francethe volume on the  Renaissance . I am reading Symonds’ “ Catholic Reaction” Finished our Titian in the evening.
